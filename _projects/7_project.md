Human creativity evaluation is labor-intensive and subjective. This project examines whether **large language models can serve as automated judges** for scientific creativity scoring, approximating the ratings human experts assign on the Scientific Creative Thinking Test (SCTT).

We systematically compare four prompting strategies — **zero-shot, few-shot, chain-of-thought, and rubric-based prompting** — measuring agreement with human raters and identifying which conditions most reliably capture the dimensions of scientific originality and elaboration.

### Research Questions

- Which prompting strategy best aligns LLM creativity judgments with human scores?
- Do chain-of-thought explanations improve rubric adherence or introduce scoring drift?
- Can a single LLM judge replace multi-rater consensus for formative feedback?
