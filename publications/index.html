publications

Publications by the Cognition and Learning Design Lab, in reverse chronological order.

2026

  1. arXiv
    Automated Domain Question Mapping (DQM) with Educational Learning Materials
    Jiho Noh, Mukhesh Raghava Katragadda, and Daesoo Lee
    arXiv preprint, 2026
    Abs arXiv Bib

    We propose an automated pipeline for mapping domain questions to educational learning materials, enabling more precise alignment between assessments and instructional content.

  2. AIED
    Using Poly-Encoders for Computationally Efficient Automated Creativity Assessment
    Sam Grouchnikov, Phillip Gregory, and Jiho Noh
    In International Conference on Artificial Intelligence in Education (AIED), 2026
    Abs Bib

    We apply Poly-Encoder architectures to automate creativity assessment in student responses, reducing reliance on expert annotation while maintaining high agreement with human raters.

2025

  1. AINA
    Investigating the Transferability and Robustness of Adversarial Attacks Between Standard and Dilated CNN Architectures
    Sachin Sharma, Jiho Noh, and Michael S. Alexiou
    In International Conference on Advanced Information Networking and Applications (AINA), 2025
    Abs Bib

    We investigate how adversarial attacks transfer between standard and dilated CNN architectures, revealing architecture-specific vulnerabilities with implications for robust model design.

  2. IEEE ICSC
    YouLeQD: Decoding the Cognitive Complexity of Questions and Engagement in Online Educational Videos from Learners’ Perspectives
    Nong Ming, Sachin Sharma, and Jiho Noh
    In IEEE International Conference on Semantic Computing (ICSC), 2025
    Abs Bib

    YouLeQD introduces a dataset and framework for analyzing the cognitive complexity of questions posed by learners in online educational videos, linking question types to engagement patterns.

  3. JEPPM
    Emerging Mental Health Challenges in Construction Industry through Web Crawling
    Zeynep Atay, Aaron Morrisett, Jiho Noh, and 1 more author
    Journal of Engineering, Project, and Production Management, 2025
    Abs Bib

    We mine web-based discourse to identify and characterize emerging mental health challenges in the construction industry, contributing a large-scale dataset and analysis framework.

  2. IJDP
